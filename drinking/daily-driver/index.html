Cocktail

A simple, reliable interpretation of the Old Fashioned that you’ll get plenty of mileage from.

  • 2 oz Bourbon*
  • ¼ oz Rich Demerara Syrup (2:1 recipe)
  • 8 dashes (8 ml) Blackstrap Bitters
  • 4 dashes (4 ml) Dry Curaçao**

Add all ingredients to a mixing glass, add ice, gently stir until properly chilled. Strain into rocks glass with a large cube. Garnish with an orange peel, taking care to express its citrus oil across the surface of the drink.

*Recommended: Elijah Craig Small Batch, Buffalo Trace, Maker’s Mark, Evan Williams Single Barrel **Note: I keep the Dry Curaçao in a bitters dasher-style bottle for this application; Recommended: Pierre Ferrand, Marie Brizard

Blackstrap Bitters

  • 2 cups Plantation OFTD Rum
  • 2 Cinnamon sticks, broken
  • 2 tsp cacao nibs
  • 2 tsp indian sarsparilla
  • 1/2 oz Allspice Dram
  • 1 tbsp dried bitter orange peel
  • 1/2 tsp mace, ground
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 2 tbsp quassia bark
  • 1/2 oz gentian extract
  • Peel of 3 limes
  • Peel of 1 orange
  • 2 oz simple syrup (1:1 white sugar recipe)

Combine all ingredients except simple syrup in a quart-sized Mason jar and let steep for 2 weeks, agitating daily. Fine-strain the bitters, pressing to extract all the liquid. Add the simple syrup in, stir to mix, and store in clean glass dasher bottles. Should keep indefinitely.
