Flat Earth

Apr 17, 2023
2 min read

Cocktail

An earthy, restorative sour with complexity that’ll make you question your own sanity.

  • 1 ½ oz Gin*
  • ¾ oz 80-proof Orange Liqueur**
  • ¾ oz Roasted Walnut Orgeat
  • ½ oz Beet Shrub
  • ½ oz lemon juice
  • 8 dashes (8 ml) Habanero Tincture

Add all ingredients to shaker, add ice, shake vigorously until properly chilled. Double-strain into a chilled coupe glass and garnish with a floating lemon wheel.

*Recommended: Vitae Modern Gin, Aviation, Botanist, Plymouth **Recommended: Vitae Orange Liqueur, Cointreau

Walnut Orgeat

  • 1 cup raw walnuts
  • hot (not boiling) water
  • 4 cups white sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ oz Orange Blossom Water
  • 1 oz vodka (to fortify)

In a dry pan, toast the walnuts over low heat to release their oils, about five minutes. Put the nuts in a blender with the sugar, salt, and 4 cups hot water. Blend on high for one minute. Fine-strain through a nut bag or cheesecloth (a cloth napkin works in a pinch), pressing to extract all of the liquid. Add the vodka and Orange Blossom Water to the strained mixture, stirring in to mix. Store in a clean container and keep refrigerated. Should keep for one month.

Beet Shrub

  • 6 beets, sliced into small pieces
  • ½ cup apple cider vinegar
  • ½ cup distilled white vinegar
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns, crushed
  • 1 tsp salt

Add the beets and both types of vinegar to a blender and blend on high for one minute until it is thoroughly pureed. Using a nutbag or cheesecloth (or cloth napkin, in a pinch), fine strain into a clean bowl. Add all other ingredients, stir to mix, and let steep overnight. Fine-strain and store in a clean container. Keep refrigerated. Should keep for one month.

Habanero Tincture

  • 1 cup blanco tequila
  • 4 habanero peppers, sliced and stemmed

Add habanero peppers to tequila in a non-reactive container. Let steep for two days. Fine-strain, then store in bitters dasher-style bottle.
